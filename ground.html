Ground Nuts


Days Instructions Audio
DAY 1 - 7 1. Prepare the soil by clearing it of weeds and debris.
2. Till the soil to loosen it up and create a smooth surface for planting.
3. Apply a balanced fertilizer such as NPK at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha and mix it well into the soil.
4. Make furrows spaced 18-24 inches apart and 1-2 inches deep in the soil.
5. Plant the groundnut seeds at a depth of 1-2 inches and 6-8 inches apart in rows.
6. Cover the seeds with soil and press down lightly to ensure good soil contact.
7. Water the seeds immediately after planting to help them germinate.
DAY 8 - 14 1. Watch for the seedlings to emerge from the soil.
2. Water the seedlings once or twice a week, depending on weather conditions, to keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.
3. Thin the seedlings to 4-6 inches apart once they are 2-3 inches tall.
4. Apply a side dressing of fertilizer such as urea or ammonium nitrate at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha.
DAY 15 - 30 1. Continue to water the seedlings as needed to keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.
2. Watch for the seedlings to develop their first true leaves.
3. Apply a second side dressing of fertilizer at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha when the plants are 4-6 inches tall.
4. Keep the area around the plants free of weeds and debris.
DAY 31 - 60 1. Watch for the groundnut plants to grow to a height of 8-10 inches.
2. Water the plants as needed to keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged.
3. Apply a third side dressing of fertilizer at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha when the plants are 8-10 inches tall.
4. Monitor the plants for signs of pests or diseases and take appropriate action if necessary.
DAY 61 - 90 1. Watch for the groundnut plants to start flowering.
2. Water the plants as needed to keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged.
3. Apply a final side dressing of fertilizer at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha when the plants are 12-14 inches tall.
4. Monitor the plants for signs of pod rot or other diseases and take appropriate action if necessary.
DAY 91 - 120 1. Watch for the groundnut plants to start forming pods.
2. Water the plants as needed to keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged.
3. Monitor the plants for signs of pests or diseases and take appropriate action if necessary.
4. Harvest the groundnuts when the leaves turn yellow and the plants begin to dry out, usually 100-120 days after planting.
5. After harvest, dry the pods in a well-ventilated area and store them in a cool, dry place.
